The Well of Time: Primary Health Centre

Overview

Location: Bhopal, Madhya Pradesh
Structure: RCC Frame with Infill Walls

"The Well of Time" reimagines the Primary Health Centre (PHC) as an environment where the emotional weight of waiting is softened through architecture. [cite_start]Rather than functioning as a purely clinical institution, the PHC becomes a gentle spatial journey — from compression to expansion to stillness[cite: 40].

Design Philosophy

The design is guided by two principles: psychological ease and functional clarity. [cite_start]The building is organized around a high, light-filled courtyard, turning it into a calm "time well" that naturally ventilates and anchors the entire plan[cite: 40].

Zoning Strategy

    [cite_start]
  • Public Zone: Registration and waiting areas placed at the front, defined by lower ceilings that slow the mind and create a grounded entry[cite: 40].
  • Shared / Community Zone: A double-height central courtyard acts as the emotional and climatic core. [cite_start]This is where time "opens up," aided by diffused light and shaded edges[cite: 40].
    • [cite_start]
  • Clinical / Private Zone: Consultation rooms and treatment areas occupy the quieter low-height wings, offering a return to comfort, privacy, and stillness[cite: 40].

Key Architectural Features

    [cite_start]
  • Height as Experience: The form transitions low → high → low, shaping a psychological gradient from tension to release to calm[cite: 40].
    • [cite_start]
  • Passive Cooling: The courtyard works as a stack-ventilation well, drawing cool air through shaded corridors and releasing warm air upward[cite: 40].
    • [cite_start]
  • Light Control: A subtle clerestory band with perforations brings in soft, non-glare daylight[cite: 40].
    • [cite_start]
  • Materiality: Locally available stone, brick, and exposed concrete to create a robust, low-maintenance public building with culturally familiar tactile quality[cite: 40].
