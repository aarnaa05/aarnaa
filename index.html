The Well of Time: Primary Health Centre

Overview

Location: Bhopal, Madhya Pradesh
Structure: RCC Frame with Infill Walls

"The Well of Time" reimagines the Primary Health Centre (PHC) as an environment where the emotional weight of waiting is softened through architecture. Rather than functioning as a purely clinical institution, the PHC becomes a gentle spatial journey — from compression to expansion to stillness.

Design Philosophy

The design is guided by two principles: psychological ease and functional clarity. The building is organized around a high, light-filled courtyard, turning it into a calm "time well" that naturally ventilates and anchors the entire plan.

Zoning Strategy

  • Public Zone: Registration and waiting areas placed at the front, defined by lower ceilings that slow the mind and create a grounded entry.
  • Shared / Community Zone: A double-height central courtyard acts as the emotional and climatic core. This is where time "opens up," aided by diffused light and shaded edges.
  • Clinical / Private Zone: Consultation rooms and treatment areas occupy the quieter low-height wings, offering a return to comfort, privacy, and stillness.

Key Architectural Features

  • Height as Experience: The form transitions low → high → low, shaping a psychological gradient from tension to release to calm.
  • Passive Cooling: The courtyard works as a stack-ventilation well, drawing cool air through shaded corridors and releasing warm air upward.
  • Light Control: A subtle clerestory band with perforations brings in soft, non-glare daylight.
  • Materiality: Locally available stone, brick, and exposed concrete to create a robust, low-maintenance public building with culturally familiar tactile quality.
